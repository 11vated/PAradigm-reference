## What Round 4 must deliver

Rounds 1–3 defined the substrate (what GSPL is, how it works, what it cannot lose to). Round 4 must define **what the substrate is filled with, what it looks like, and what users feel.** Specifically:

1. **Look-and-feel.** GSPL's visual identity, design language, studio IDE interaction design, and the choreographed "woah" moments that make a first-time user feel they have crossed a threshold.

2. **The inventory of reality.** Every gseed needs primitives. Chemistry, physics constants, materials, particles and fields, biology, anatomy, geology, weather, natural phenomena. None of these are placeholders; they are databases curated to scientific accuracy and packaged as substrate-native primitives that any engine can compose.

3. **Rendering completeness.** A coverage atlas of every visual phenomenon GSPL must be able to express, mapped to the engines that produce it. The qualitative expectation at 100% potential — what "everything renders" actually means.

4. **Cross-art-style character canon.** A character defined once in GSPL must be expressible in any art style — pixel art, anime, photoreal, watercolor, claymation, cubism, ukiyo-e, voxel — without losing identity. The substrate's answer to "draw my character in 50 styles."

5. **Universal anything-to-gseed conversion.** A pipeline that turns *anything* — a photograph, a song, a paragraph of text, a 3D scan, a real-world video, a hand sketch, a chemical formula, a geographic coordinate, a personality description, a memory — into a renderable, lineage-bearing gseed.

6. **Agent web cross-reference.** When a user mentions anything ("a 1972 Lamborghini Miura," "Studio Ghibli's Howl," "the smell of petrichor"), GSPL's agent autonomously gathers references — images, descriptions, related works, scientific data — from the web, attributes them, scores them for quality, and presents them as candidate inputs to the user's gseed.

7. **The knowledge graph.** A unified, federated reference database architecture: chemistry, physics, materials, biology, geography, art history, character archetypes, audio, music theory, color science, and more. Licensed, attributed, versioned, federated.

8. **Zero placeholders, zero mockups, zero global variables.** Every primitive in every database is real, sourced, attributed, and verifiable. The substrate refuses to ship any layer that depends on hand-waved data.

The directive is total: GSPL's inventory must span **every domain a user might ever invoke**, sourced from authoritative open databases, with real data and zero placeholders.

## Method

Same posture as Round 3. No "doesn't." No mockups. No global variables. No placeholders. Every primitive is sourced from a real, attributable database. Every "woah" moment has a design rationale and a measurable success criterion. Every art style is real, named, and has reference works. Every chemistry molecule comes from PubChem with a CID. Every physics constant comes from CODATA with an uncertainty bound. Every BRDF is fitted to real measured data.

## Confidence target

Each brief targets 4/5 confidence with audited primary sources. The inventory briefs (081-086) target 5/5 because their data is sourced from authoritative open scientific databases — there is no judgment call, just curation.

## Brief template (unchanged from Round 3)

Question / Why it matters / What we know from the spec / Findings / Risks / Recommendation / Confidence / Spec impact / Open follow-ups / Sources

## Output volume estimate

14 briefs + plan + synthesis. Approximately the size of Round 2's Tier I plus all of Round 3 combined. The inventory briefs will carry substantial reference data; the look-and-feel briefs will be more design-prose-heavy.

## What Round 4 does NOT do

- It does not change the substrate's architectural commitments. Round 3 closed those.
- It does not pick a launch date. That belongs in `roadmap/`.
- It does not write code. It writes the curated inputs that future code will consume.
- It does not commit to specific art styles or specific characters. It defines the *system* by which any character and any style can be expressed.

## Deliverable

Phase 1 of GSPL ships with a substrate that is **substantively filled** — not "we will add chemistry later," but "here are 50 elements with full periodic table data, here are 200 molecules with bond structures, here is the BRDF library with 100 measured materials, here is the anatomy library with full human and mammal skeletons, here is the weather system with realistic atmospheric scattering." On top of that filled substrate, the studio IDE has an unmistakable visual identity, the choreographed woah moments work, and the agent can cross-reference anything a user mentions.

This is the round where GSPL stops being a beautiful architecture and becomes a beautiful, fully-stocked, ready-to-use creative reality.

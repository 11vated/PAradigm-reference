## The substrate's constitutional commitments (Round 4)

These are the **non-patchable** commitments the substrate makes to its users:

1. **No silent guesses.** Every measured value carries a confidence score; unknowns are unbound, not hand-waved (INV-348).
2. **No living-person gseeds in the foundation namespace** (INV-343).
3. **No copyrighted derivatives without explicit fair-use attestation** (INV-350).
4. **No trademarked-specific named gseeds in the foundation namespace** (INV-335, INV-350).
5. **No sacred-restricted cultural symbols without source-culture attribution and respect** (INV-333, INV-350).
6. **No diagnostic claims about identifiable persons** (INV-339, INV-350).
7. **No weapon manufacturing schematics** (INV-350).
8. **No hallucination by the agent** — the agent can compose only nodes the graph proves exist, or it surfaces the gap (INV-357).
9. **Forever-signed credit lineage** on every authored gseed (Brief 078 INV-303, carried into Round 4 by Brief 088).
10. **Tombstone deletion preserves lineage** so credit cannot be erased even when content is removed (INV-356).
11. **Mechanics yes, trademark no** for character power systems — every mechanic of every famous power system is a `power://` substrate primitive in the foundation namespace; trademarked-specific named characters are creator-namespace only and never signed under the foundation identity (INV-365).
12. **Mechanics yes, studio no** for media — every craft technique of every famous studio is a `studio-technique://` and `art-framework://` substrate primitive in the foundation namespace; trademarked named studios, films, TV shows, and characters are creator-namespace only (INV-374).
13. **The substrate is open by construction** — anything a creator imagines that the substrate doesn't already cover composes through the `ext://` open extension protocol (INV-378), the foundation promotion pathway (INV-379), and the "I forgot to ask for it" agent workflow (INV-380), without ever requiring a foundation patch and without weakening the constitutional layer (INV-381).

These are not moderation features. They are substrate-level constitutional commitments that cannot be patched out without forking the foundation identity.

## What a creator experiences after Round 4

A new user opens the GSPL studio (Brief 079) and:

1. **Sees the armory** — 1,000 canonical signed seeds across every category, each composed of measured library primitives, each forkable in one click, each carrying its full composition graph as a learning surface.
2. **Asks the agent** — "make a Heian-era courtesan writing a letter by candlelight, in watercolor, then again in 3D-stylized" — and gets two coherent grounded scenes citing every source.
3. **Uploads a phone photo** of their grandmother's locket and watches the conversion pipeline negotiate every property with them: silver or pewter? oxidized or polished? — and walks away with a signed, lineage-bearing gseed they can compose into anything.
4. **Mentions** "1920s Hindustani court musician" and watches the agent fetch references from the British Library, V&A, and Tasveer Ghar at Tier 1, ground them against the wardrobe and instrument libraries, and surface every ungrounded field for negotiation.
5. **Forks** an armory seed, modifies it, signs it, and either keeps it private or publishes it to the federation — joining the substrate's collective memory.
6. **Time-travels** the substrate's history, scrubbing across 200 years, six continents, twelve art styles, every weather condition, every material aging trajectory.

The substrate is no longer a library. It is **a creative organism with a measured world inside it**.

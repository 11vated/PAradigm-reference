# Round 5 Synthesis — From Locked Architecture to First Thousand Users

## What Round 5 was for

Round 5 answered a single directive: **the locked Round 4 architecture must become a foundation creators can actually live inside, without any of the 13 constitutional commitments bending, any of the 72 Round 4 inventions degrading into marketing copy, or any layer of the stack revealing a placeholder.**

Rounds 1–3 defined what GSPL is. Round 4 filled the substrate with the stuff of the world and locked the architecture at confidence 4.5/5. Round 5 turns the locked design into ship-readiness — curation effort, partner engagements, operational substrate, and the discipline to launch a foundation a solo founder can actually run.

Round 5 introduced **no new substrate primitives**, **no new URL schemes for substrate content**, and **no new constitutional commitments**. Every brief operates inside the locked architecture and adds executional rigor on top.

## What a solo founder can do on Monday morning

Round 5 is not aspirational. The first executable week looks concrete:

1. Begin **Foundation Kernel curation** against the Brief 095 constraint stack — first 5 seeds across 5 categories, signed under the founder identity.
2. Send **Wave 1 partner outreach** (Brief 098 Template T1-O) to 8 Tier-1 institutions.
3. Stand up the **CI grounding gate** scaffold (Brief 097 INV-397) so that every subsequent seed and adapter passes through it.
4. Draft the **9-seat council bylaws** (Brief 107) with placeholder seats for the founder and the first technical steward.
5. Publish the **year-1 roadmap** (Brief 108) as the public substrate commitment.

Every action above maps to a named brief, a named invention, and a named gate.

## Confidence

**4.5/5 for Round 5 as a whole.** The design layer of Round 5 is at the same confidence as Round 4 (5/5 within the envelope of what is knowable at plan time). The execution layer carries lower confidence (3.5–4/5) because partner response rates, solo-founder bandwidth, and live-cohort retention against the 8-minute path are unknown until measured. The drop list (INV-479), the risk register (INV-478), and the sign-up pause (INV-458) are the structural defenses that hold the substrate honest under that uncertainty.

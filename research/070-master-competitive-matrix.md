## The headline summary

**Out of 20 axes, GSPL is unequivocally first on 7, tied for first on 11, and trailing on 2 (raw quality, first-run polish тАФ both closing).**

No competitor matches GSPL on more than ~6 axes simultaneously. The closest matchers are:
- **ComfyUI:** 9 axes (local, open, substrate flexibility) but no provenance, no marketplace, no federation, no conversational interface, no critique loop, no evolution.
- **Godot:** 8 axes (local, open, IP-clean) but no AI, no lineage, no provenance, no federation, no marketplace.
- **Stable Diffusion ecosystem:** 7 axes (local, open) but single-modality, no lineage, no provenance, no marketplace.

**No competitor combines sovereignty + multi-modal + lineage + provenance + marketplace + federation + critique + evolution.** That bundle is the GSPL definition.

## The "structural lock" claim

GSPL's competitive advantages are *constitutional*, not features. A competitor cannot adopt them without ceasing to be themselves:
- **Hosted competitors** (MJ, Sora, Veo, Inworld, Roblox) cannot become local-first without abandoning their business model.
- **Engine competitors** (Unity, Unreal) cannot become substrate-level without rewriting their core.
- **Open-weight competitors** (SD, Flux) cannot add lineage, marketplace, federation, or critique without becoming a different product.
- **Procedural competitors** (Houdini, Blender) cannot add AI as substrate without retrofitting onto a closed/incompatible architecture.
- **Workflow competitors** (ComfyUI) cannot add federation or marketplace without becoming a hosted platform.

This is the moat. It is not a feature lead. It is an architectural lead.

## Honest weaknesses

GSPL is *not* universally superior. The honest weaknesses are:

1. **Raw aesthetic quality at v1.** Trails MJ and Sora. Closes as backbones improve.
2. **First-time polish.** MJ has years of UX iteration; GSPL is starting.
3. **Audience size.** Picbreeder had ~thousands; Roblox has 70M; GSPL has zero. This is a marketing problem more than a product problem, but it's real.
4. **Specialized excellence.** Houdini for VFX, Unreal for AAA, Inworld for hosted NPCs тАФ GSPL does not beat them in their specialty.
5. **Multiplayer runtime.** GSPL doesn't have one and won't.
6. **Compute requirements.** Local execution needs a real GPU for the best engines. Hybrid mode (Brief 055) is the mitigation.
7. **Open-weight licensing volatility.** GSPL's quality depends on what licensing permits.

These are accepted, articulated, and have mitigations.

## Risks identified

- **The matrix oversimplifies.** Each axis has nuance. Mitigation: this matrix is a summary; the per-rival briefs (063-069) carry the depth.
- **Axes are arguable.** Different observers will weight them differently. Mitigation: the matrix is published; users can re-weight.
- **GSPL has to actually deliver.** The matrix is a promise until v1 ships.
- **Competitors will respond.** Likely additions: Unity Muse expanding; Civitai adding signing; Stability adding lineage. None can copy the full bundle.
